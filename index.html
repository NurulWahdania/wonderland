Raja Ampat
Raja Ampat, which means "Four Kings" in Indonesian, is an archipelago located off the northwest tip of Bird's Head Peninsula on the island of New Guinea, in Indonesia's West Papua province. It is renowned for its extraordinary marine biodiversity and stunning natural beauty.

Mandala Suci Wenara Wana
Mandala Suci Wenara Wana, better known as the Ubud Monkey Forest, is a nature reserve and Hindu temple complex located in Ubud, Bali. This place is famous for its free-roaming population of long-tailed macaques and their interactions with visitors. The forest also holds high spiritual and cultural value for the local community.

Mount Ijen
Mount Ijen is located in East Java and is renowned for the "blue fire" phenomenon in its crater. Visitors can witness blue flames emerging from burning sulfur gas. Ijen is also known for its turquoise crater lake and the traditional sulfur miners who work there.

Mount Bromo
Mount Bromo is one of the most iconic volcanoes in Indonesia, situated in the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park, East Java. The sunrise view at Bromo is world-famous, featuring an active volcano backdrop and vast sea of sand. Visitors can hike to the peak or ride horses to explore the surrounding area.

Nusa Penida
Nusa Penida is an island located southeast of Bali. The island is famous for its exotic beaches, dramatic cliffs, and unique rock formations. Some popular spots in Nusa Penida include Kelingking Beach with its T-Rex shaped cliff, Angel's Billabong, and Broken Beach.

Mount Rinjani
Mount Rinjani is the second highest active volcano in Indonesia, located on Lombok Island. Rinjani offers a challenging hiking experience with spectacular views. At 2,000 meters altitude lies Segara Anak Lake, a beautiful crater lake. The mountain also holds spiritual significance for local residents and is often used as a pilgrimage site.
